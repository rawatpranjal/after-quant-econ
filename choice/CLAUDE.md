# Choice & Demand Models

## Models in this section
Discrete choice theory, revealed preference, Bayesian learning,
and demand basics (logit and nested logit).

## Common patterns
- Afriat inequalities and GARP testing for revealed preference
- Use transitive closure and Bronars-style power checks inside the Afriat
  tutorial when needed; do not maintain a separate Warshall-only active page.
- Maximum likelihood estimation for logit/nested logit models
- Berry inversion: log(s_j) - log(s_0) = X*beta - alpha*p + xi
- IV/2SLS estimation to handle price endogeneity

## Key economics
- Revealed preference: can observed choices be rationalized by utility maximization?
- Discrete choice: logit model for probabilistic choice over alternatives
- Bayesian learning: how agents update beliefs from signals
- Demand basics: logit and nested logit
- IIA property and how nested logit relaxes it
- Endogeneity: why OLS underestimates price sensitivity
